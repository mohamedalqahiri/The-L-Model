# The L-Exponential Model: Physical Foundations and PROSUM.A Function

**Mohamed Al-Qahiri, PhD**

**Working Paper — June 2026**

## Abstract

This paper introduces the L-Exponential Model, an alternative to classical scarcity-based economics. Built on the integrated production-consumption function PROSUM.A, the model demonstrates that human innovation serves as the sole sovereign factor of production once humanity is liberated from routine labor through deep robotics and fluid material flows.

The paper presents a 20-year dynamic simulation (2026–2046) demonstrating output explosion, attainment of absolute abundance, and dissolution of rent-seeking mechanisms.

**Keywords:** L-Exponential Model, PROSUM.A Function, Creatocracy, Flow Efficiency, Dynamic Simulation.

## 1. Introduction and Philosophical Framework

Humanity's right is to enjoy an optimal state where human will is liberated, immediate, and effective without friction. Absolute abundance is the pathway to existential emancipation through three structural pillars:

1. **Human Capacity** - Restoring human intelligence as the ultimate value-generating flow
2. **Technical Capacity** - Shifting from extractive stocks to instantaneous, abundant flows
3. **Deep Robotics** - Converting machinery into seamless execution channels

## 2. Mathematical Architecture of PROSUM.A

```
PROSUM.A_t = L_t^(1 + (sync(r_t, f_t)/(1+R_t+σ_t)))

Where:
...
- PROSUM.A_t = System output at time t
- L_t = Active human innovation factor
- ‹1› in the exponent = Inherent Cognitive Constant.
- sync_t = Synchronization function (f_t, r_t)
- R_t = Responsiveness and Knowledge Development Vector
- σ_t = Institutional and sectoral drag coefficient
```

### Human Innovation Factor (L_t)

The active creative cohort represents 27% of population, growing as:

```
L_t = L_0 × exp(n × t)
Where: n = 0.015 × (r_t / f_t + 1) ∈ [0.03, 0.035]
```

### Synchronization Function

```
sync_t = f_t × (1 + ln(1+ r_t/f_t))
At full abundance (r=f=1): sync → 1+ln(2) ≈ 1.693
```

### Flow and Robotics Efficiency

```
f_t = 1 / (1 + exp(-k × (t - t_f)))
r_t = 1 / (1 + exp(-k × (t - t_r)))
Where k = 0.65 year^-1
```

## 3. Structural Surplus Absorption

### Responsiveness and Knowledge Development (R_t)

```
R_t = θ × (f_t / r_t), where θ = 0.32

Distributed as:
- Human-Core Diagnostics: 31.0%
- Extra-Terrestrial Infrastructure: 12.5%
- Cyber-Protection: 18.75%
- Infrastructure & Fusion: 37.5%

### Institutional and Sectoral Drag Coefficient (σ_t)

```
- σ_t = 0.20×(1 + D_sec)×e^(λ×(1–f_t)+ γ × G)
  Where 0.20 = Non-reducible institutional inhibition;  λ = 0.30.
- D_sec = (1–f_t)×[τ×|2ρ – 1| + μ×d_cagr].
```
Measures bureaucratic and sectoral resistance to flow liquidity.
Where D_sec isolates the structural drag divergence between high-tech ecosystems and slower primary sectors:
size variance weight τ = 0.15, growth variance weight μ = 0.5, relative high-tech sector volume ρ = 0.35, and current sector growth rate differential d_cagr = 7\%. Government artificial preservation of scarcity frameworks is captured via (G = 0.25) with a direct impact weight of γ  = 0.2.
